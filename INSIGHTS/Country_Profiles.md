# COUNTRY PROFILES — Global Economic Trends Analysis

# India — Economic Profile

## GDP Growth

India’s GDP growth experienced multiple phases of expansion and slowdown over the observed period.

### Key Metrics

* Peak GDP Growth: approximately 8–9%
* Lowest GDP Growth: approximately –6% in 2020
* Typical Growth Range: 5–8%

### Key Insights

* Strong growth phase during 2003–2008.
* Sharp contraction occurred in 2020 due to COVID-19 disruptions.
* India remained one of the strongest long-term growth performers among emerging economies.

---

## Inflation

### Key Metrics

* Highest Inflation: approximately 11.9–12%
* Lowest Inflation: approximately 4%
* Inflation in 2020: approximately 6.8%

### Key Insights

* India experienced two major inflation cycles.
* Inflation remained consistently higher than most developed economies.
* Inflation volatility reflected rapid economic expansion and supply-side pressures.

---

## Trade Openness

### Key Metrics

* 1995: approximately 21%
* Peak: approximately 55–56%
* 2020: approximately 39–40%

### Key Insights

* Trade openness increased by nearly 167% between 1995 and 2012.
* India gradually integrated into global trade networks after liberalization.
* Trade declined moderately after 2013 due to global slowdown and domestic adjustments.

---

## Unemployment

### Key Metrics

* 1995: approximately 2.2%
* 2020: approximately 7.9%
* Relative Rise: approximately 259%

### Key Insights

* Unemployment remained stable for nearly two decades.
* Significant structural increase appeared after 2015.
* COVID-19 sharply worsened labor market conditions.

---

## CO₂ Emissions

### Key Metrics

* 1995: approximately 0.75%
* Peak: approximately 1.78%
* 2020: approximately 1.55%

### Key Insights

* Emissions more than doubled due to industrialization and rising energy demand.
* Slight decline after 2018 reflected policy measures and COVID-related slowdown.

---

## School Enrollment

### Key Metrics

* 1995: approximately 45%
* 2020: approximately 79%
* Relative Increase: approximately 74%

### Key Insights

* Major educational progress occurred during 2000–2010.
* Government programs significantly improved access to education.

---

## Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)

### Key Metrics

* 1995: approximately 0.35%
* 2020: approximately 2.35%
* Increase: more than 6×

### Key Insights

* Liberalization policies improved investor confidence.
* India became a major emerging-market investment destination.

---

# China — Economic Profile

## GDP Growth

### Key Metrics

* Peak GDP Growth: approximately 14–15%
* Typical Growth Range (1990s–2000s): 8–14%
* Growth in late 2010s: approximately 2–6%

### Key Insights

* China recorded the highest long-term growth among all analyzed economies.
* Growth accelerated rapidly after economic reforms and WTO entry.
* Growth gradually slowed after 2010 as the economy matured.

---

## Inflation

### Key Metrics

* Peak Inflation: approximately 24%
* Post-2000 Inflation Range: mostly 0–6%

### Key Insights

* China experienced extreme inflation volatility in the mid-1990s.
* Inflation stabilized significantly after 2000.
* The country demonstrated strong long-term inflation control.

---

## Trade Openness

### Key Metrics

* 1990: approximately 25%
* Peak: approximately 60–65%

### Key Insights

* Trade openness rose sharply after WTO accession in 2001.
* China became one of the world’s largest export-driven economies.
* Trade dependence moderated slightly after 2010.

---

## Unemployment

### Key Metrics

* Mostly remained between 1–5%

### Key Insights

* China maintained relatively low and stable unemployment.
* Employment stability reflected strong industrial and manufacturing growth.

---

## CO₂ Emissions

### Key Metrics

* Strong rise throughout 2000s and 2010s

### Key Insights

* Emissions increased rapidly due to industrialization and manufacturing expansion.
* China became one of the world’s largest emitters during its growth boom.

## School Enrollment

## Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)
